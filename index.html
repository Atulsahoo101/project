He is one of the few people who still stays at Satabhaya.

Mr. Prabhakar Behera

Due to regular [ almost yearly ] severe  cyclones, the shoreline shifted more inward towards the land and eroded away more than   600   households with 5-6 members per household getting displaced and many died as well.

Mark the temple and nearby settlements getting eroded away more severely along with the year of cyclones. People also call them the vanishing villages .

Source for all the above images in this section : Google Earth Pro.

As evident from the timelapse section, episodic cyclones have caused immense infrastructural damages including a significant cultural symbol of Satabhaya, the 150 year old Maa Panchubarahi Temple . which was once a shared space of belief, but today it has been lost to erosion. It’s ruins like the door, bricks, columns etc, are washed way a few meters away from the temple location.
Over the course of time, the temple was used as a reference point to keep track of the shoreline ingression by the villagers.

Another hallmark of the event, a handpump that was once surrounded by settlements, is now of no use. The temple was used as a reference point to keep track of the shoreline ingression similarly, villagers used handpumps to keep track of receding land caused due to coastal erosion.

The people of Satabhaya hold beliefs beyond structures, as seen through a person who still places offering to the gods near the ruins of the temple.
